Clear guide

The plan in clear, practical terms

Cartilage

Cartilage is cushion material. It helps joints move smoothly.

Sciatica

Sciatica is irritation of a nerve that often starts in the lower back and travels into the leg.

Exercise goal

Make the muscles stronger so the joint and nerve have less stress.

Best style

Slow, low-impact, controlled exercises that are easy to repeat consistently.

Step 1

Start with the pain map, not the body part name.

Cartilage pain and nerve pain can both show up in the leg, but they behave differently. Use this section to choose the right emphasis for the workouts.

Knee cartilage / meniscus pattern

Pain around the knee. Worse with stairs, squats, twisting, or standing from a low chair. May swell, click, catch, or feel stiff.

Hip cartilage pattern

Deep ache in groin, side hip, buttock, or thigh. Worse after walking, standing, stairs, or getting out of a car.

Ankle / foot cartilage pattern

Pain around ankle or foot joints. Worse with long standing, uneven ground, steps, or shoes with poor support.

Sciatica / back-disc pattern

Pain, tingling, burning, or numbness traveling from low back or butt down the leg. Often worse sitting, bending, coughing, or lifting.

Step 2

The complete 12-week plan.

This is the backbone of the website. It combines joint-friendly strength, low-impact cardio, spine/nerve calming, mobility, and weight-friendly progression.

01

Calm + control

Goal: reduce fear, reduce flare-ups, learn the movements, and stop the cycle of pain → rest → weakness → more pain.

  • Strength: 2–3 days/week, easy range only.
  • Cardio: 5–12 minutes, 4–5 days/week.
  • Best choices: low-impact indoor marching, stationary bike, flat walking.
  • Use: Workout A + Workout C + short cardio.
02

Build the support system

Goal: make thighs, hips, glutes, calves, and core strong enough to unload joints and calm irritated nerves.

  • Strength: 3 days/week.
  • Cardio: 12–25 minutes, 4–5 days/week.
  • Add: low step-ups, balance, gentle hip hinge.
  • Use: Workouts A, B, C, and optional Home Cardio Day.
03

Strength for real life

Goal: easier stairs, longer walks, better balance, less joint load, and less flare-up risk.

  • Strength: 3 days/week with controlled progress.
  • Cardio: build toward 150 minutes/week if tolerated.
  • Progress: one variable at a time — reps, set, step height, or band.
  • Use: all workouts, rotating by symptoms.

Progress rules

Make it harder only when the body earns it.

Progress should feel steady and manageable. The best rehab is often not dramatic — it is repeatable.

01

One change at a time

Add reps, or add a set, or add resistance. Never all three in the same week.

02

Next-day check

If pain or swelling is worse the next day, the previous session was too much.

03

Range before load

First move well. Then move more. Then add resistance.

04

Cardio without pounding

Low-impact indoor marching, stationary cycling, elliptical, and flat walking beat jumping and running early on.

Safety

Stop signs are part of the plan.

This plan is educational. It does not replace a doctor or physical therapist. Use extra caution with diabetes, osteoporosis, heart disease, recent injury, severe arthritis, neurological symptoms, or unexplained weight loss.

Get medical help quickly if:

  • New bowel or bladder problems.
  • Numbness in the groin/saddle area.
  • New serious leg weakness or foot drop.
  • Joint is hot, very swollen, red, or feverish.
  • Cannot bear weight, the knee locks, or pain follows a fall/twist.
  • Pain keeps worsening after 2–4 weeks of sensible changes.
