01 / ORIENTATION

Pain map

Read the pattern before choosing the movement.

Joint irritation and nerve pain can both appear in the leg, but they respond differently. Pick the pattern that sounds most like your day.

01LOCAL / JOINT

Knee cartilage or meniscus pattern

Pain around the knee, often worse with stairs, squats, twisting, or rising from a low chair. It may swell, click, catch, or feel stiff.

02DEEP / JOINT

Hip cartilage pattern

A deep ache in the groin, side hip, buttock, or thigh—often worse after walking, standing, stairs, or getting out of a car.

03LOAD / JOINT

Ankle or foot cartilage pattern

Pain around the ankle or foot joints, commonly worse with long standing, uneven ground, steps, or poorly supportive shoes.

04TRAVELING / NERVE

Sciatica or back-disc pattern

Burning, tingling, numbness, or pain traveling from the low back or buttock down the leg—often worse sitting, bending, coughing, or lifting.

05 / PROGRESSION

The next-day rule

Make it harder only when the body earns it.

Good progression is almost boring: a little more capacity, no dramatic spike, and a body that still agrees with the decision tomorrow.

01

One change at a time

Add reps, a set, resistance, or range—never all four in the same week.

02

Check tomorrow

If pain, swelling, or limping is clearly worse the next day, the previous dose was too high.

03

Range before load

First move well. Then move farther. Only then add resistance.

04

Cardio without pounding

Marching, cycling, elliptical work, and flat walking usually beat jumping and running early on.

06 / SAFETY

Stop signs belong inside the plan.

Some symptoms need care, not another exercise.

This guide is educational and does not replace a doctor or physical therapist. Use extra caution with diabetes, osteoporosis, heart disease, recent injury, severe arthritis, neurological symptoms, or unexplained weight loss.

URGENT REVIEWRED FLAGS / 06

Get medical help quickly if you notice:

  • 01New bowel or bladder problems.
  • 02Numbness in the groin or saddle area.
  • 03New serious leg weakness or foot drop.
  • 04A hot, very swollen, red, or feverish joint.
  • 05Inability to bear weight, a locked knee, or pain after a fall or twist.
  • 06Pain that keeps worsening after 2–4 weeks of sensible changes.
